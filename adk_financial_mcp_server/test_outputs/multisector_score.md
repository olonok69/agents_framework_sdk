# Multi-Sector Score-Model-Driven Analysis: Technology vs. Financials (1Y, Aggressive Risk Profile)

## Executive Summary

A score-model-driven comparison of the Technology and Financials sectors over the past year reveals that Financials exhibited a less negative aggregate excess return (-4.7%) than Technology (-9.5%). Financials also produced the highest individual signal (+85.9% for BAC). However, all Financials constituents are rated AVOID by the MCP model, indicating elevated risk or insufficient conviction for exposure, even under an aggressive risk profile. Technology offers one HOLD-rated opportunity (AAPL, +20.7% best signal), while the remainder are AVOID. These findings suggest that, despite some high-magnitude signals in Financials, the model does not currently endorse sector allocation to Financials. Technology presents slightly more model conviction but with lower upside potential.

## Methodology

This analysis utilizes the MCP’s unified market scanner in score-model-driven mode. Sectors are compared using aggregate score quality (average excess return) and signal consistency (distribution of ratings and best signals). The aggressive risk profile allows for consideration of higher volatility and momentum, but model ratings (HOLD/AVOID) remain the primary determinant of sector conviction. No strategy-voting or allocation recommendations are made; the focus is on comparative score analysis.

### Score Synthesis

- **Aggregate Score Quality:** Financials’ average excess return (-4.7%) is less negative than Technology’s (-9.5%), indicating relatively better sector-wide performance, though both are negative.
- **Signal Strength:** BAC’s +85.9% best signal is the highest across both sectors, but the AVOID rating signals model-identified risk or lack of conviction. Technology’s top signal (AAPL +20.7%) is strong and is accompanied by a HOLD rating, reflecting some model support.
- **Consistency:** Technology offers one HOLD (AAPL), while Financials’ top signals are all rated AVOID. This suggests that, despite high-magnitude signals in Financials, the model does not find sufficient consistency or reliability to warrant exposure.
- **Risk Concentration:** The aggressive risk profile is most evident in Financials, where signal dispersion is high (from +85.9% to -4.5%). However, the uniform AVOID ratings indicate that the sector’s risk is currently too concentrated or volatile for model-based allocation, even for aggressive investors.

## Conclusion

- **Ranking by Aggregate Score Quality and Consistency:**
    1. **Financials**: Less negative aggregate excess return and the highest individual signal, but all constituents are rated AVOID, indicating elevated risk and lack of model conviction.
    2. **Technology**: More negative aggregate excess return, but offers one HOLD-rated opportunity (AAPL), suggesting slightly more model support, albeit with lower upside potential.

- **Risk Caveats:**  
  The high dispersion of signals in Financials, combined with uniform AVOID ratings, highlights significant risk concentration and potential for false positives. Technology’s single HOLD (AAPL) offers a marginally more stable opportunity, but overall sector conviction remains low.

- **Interpretation for Aggressive Risk Profile:**  
  While the aggressive risk profile allows for consideration of higher volatility and faster sector rotation, the MCP model’s current ratings do not endorse exposure to Financials due to risk concentration and lack of consistency. Technology presents a modest opportunity via AAPL but does not offer broad sector support.
